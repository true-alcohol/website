The question of being has always arrived dressed. What we call the subject is
not a substance that later enters relations, but a knot of relations that
afterwards imagines itself a substance. To speak of a *socio-metaphysical* being
is to refuse the choice between the two — to insist that the metaphysical and the
social are not two floors of one house but a single surface folded against itself.

> Solidarity, all too solidarity; a De Sade stands, where pity encompasses the
> sexual tendency that the bourgeois has for the victim.

## The undressing

Consider what happens when we strip the subject of its predicates. Nothing
remains — and yet the *nothing* is not empty. It is structured, oriented, sexed.

## Coda

The essay ends where it began: with a being that cannot appear except by being
dressed, and cannot think except by undressing. *True Alcohol* is the name for
