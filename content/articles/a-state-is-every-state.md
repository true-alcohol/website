# **Society Must Be Defended** **_Against Itself_** 

Notes on the January 26’ Movement 

## **A State is Every State** 

A state is not a particular conscious territory, government, or regime, for it is a form, a form that repeats itself when there is a stabilization of power. A state is a reappearing spectrum of certain interpretations of rationality experiencing itself through a version of environment and culture. The state is a tendency, it is a neutralization of escape. The elements that constantly reappear in states are (1) classification of population, (2) an idea of the _Other_ , the _Stranger_ , and (3) the thresholds of legitimacy that the state offers. The tendency of the state-form creates, as mentioned before, certain senses of logic, and genocide as a deviant act is immanent for it does not necessarily contradict the notions. 

Genocide, starvation, repression as a whole is not a breakdown of order, it is not a leak outside of state rationality, it is the mainstream itself. As mentioned by Zygmunt Bauman, genocide is enabled by several aspects of modernity, that one at the center being a logistfication of problems. At no point in the process of massacre does the system admit _murder_ with the qualities the term contains. Instead, the system understands itself as an approach to solve a logistical problem. A state is every state; just as Freud believed in the universality of the Oedipus complex, every state bears a structural disposition toward elimination because every population is _in principle_ capable of threatening the monopoly of violence, every population is capable of refusing loyalty. Has any state ever approached genocide against the _Stranger_ as _strange_ as cannibalism? It is certainly not a deviation, it is a mere manifestation of the rational for the act itself is rationalized. Dividing labor is a _tic_ that industrialization has due to its anxiety regarding efficiency. Modernity and modern state-form as it is currently known follows the same procedure in repression; a moral 

alibi is created, there is structural innocence in all microbes that participated. A railway clerk optimizing timetables is the state, a nurse regularly assigned to counter-protest forces is the state, while all believe that their task is technical, not a moral one. State rationality embedded itself into modernity and reappears as _the_ rational through an unconscious sense of helplessness existing within the microbes. What leaks outside of these divisions? The feminine. 

## **The** **_Feminine_ does not have Organs.** 

The body is composed of a categorical mode of thought, the organs being a manifestation of them. Yet, what is _Being_ before the formation of the strata, the category, the box? It is what Deleuze would call a Body without Organs (BwO), a delay in the formation (or abortion, depending on how you read Deleuze and Gauttari) of the body. The body that leaks outside of the flow of the state’s consciousness. It is simply what fails, or refuses, to be annexed by categories. 

It is only when immanence is no longer immanence to anything other than itself that we can speak of a plane of immanence. 

We want to prove our existence; a social recognition, an approval, and this approval remains within the figure of the _Woman_ . That is, the figure where the contradiction of the state’s mediation reveals itself on. The woman’s body is the leak, the full manifestation of whatever the children of 26’ ask for; it is the literal production of deliberate desire, that is, the desire to wear another skin. I believe that here we differ with the Western man’s model of resistance; Resistance is a repositioning of desire in the Western life, as in, resistance uses the existing free space to create, vote against and for, and so on and so forth. The _Oriental_ Resistant here is not repositioning her desire for she is ontologically affirming the existence of her desire in front of the state. Truly, what stops me from extracting the _whole_ in her image? _What stops me from_ 

_seeing her hair?_ In the image of _the Oriental Woman_ , she does not want to wear another skin, she wants to have one to begin with. 

Unlike other waves, this one cannot ignore the fact that it is pregnant with a daughter. I would doubt whether any movement ever captured the full design of its will to power in a figure as feminine as this one. 

## **Should the Resistance Have an Above?** 

The negation of multiplicity in resistance is the machine that allows reterritorialization even after a movement that aimed for deterritorialization. There is an essentialist point of view within resistance that negates the existential, that negates the multiplicity in the individual’s life itself. A taboofication of certain behaviors in resistance by the resistance itself would set it back further than the actual authority; in other terms, the constant search of a soul in a movement is unnecessary, for a movement is a micro-revolution only when it cannot realize itself, when it cannot look at the mirror. There is a fever in the case that we see here, the fever of a unity within the sea of revolutionary thought. And this fever will not necessarily affect any perception to reach any  middle grounds other than a front that is contained with multiplicity. 

Here, then, after the unfoldings, can one imagine the Man in front of the motorcycles as an individual instead of an image? Can one not imagine him as a line of flight? Can one not imagine him as a thesis instead of a synthesis? 

There is a vision within the _Asphalt Man_ , that is the possibility of affirming other possibilities. Let us go into Gilles Deleuze as far as we can, where there is resistance, there at almost all times a potential for a desire to arise which seeks a re-capture of the body. But even though many of us admit this, did we ever ask ourselves why do we even desire this recapture, or in Deleuzian terms, reterritorilization? The notion of a reachable _Absolute_ , I believe, causes this rush, this need for One founding moment, One seizure of power, One decisive revolution. But is there a necessary need for a revolution to be contained with decisiveness? The Asphalt Man should not be treated as such, for it should be seen as a permanent micro-revolutionary, an eternal transvaluation of gender, knowledge, labor, and sexuality. The ethical criterion that one has to refer to here is, does x increase or decrease our collective ability to act? Does x multiply relations? Then, unity should not overrule multiplicity. No structure should be allowed to become unquestionable, it is then here that it is _forbidden to forbid._
