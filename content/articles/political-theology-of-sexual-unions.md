# **The Political Theology of Sexual Unions** 

_Now the labyrinth is no longer a labyrinth of knowledge and morality, a path entered while holding the thread by the one who goes to kill the bull. The labyrinth has transformed into Dionysus: “I am your labyrinth.”_ 

## **Introduction** 

If, as Žižek rightly says, we are a people of decaffeinated coffee, non-alcoholic beer, and sex without sex, then perhaps it would be more accurate to say that we are monogamous without the one, and polygamous without the many. Besides being the cell of society, the family is also its curse; it reproduces itself beyond the borders of the apartment as well, and thus the premature collapse of this institution would mean a meaningless step, one that would only reproduce the same structure on even more terrifying levels. 

Perhaps, as Kafka did, it is necessary to push the family to the absolute, rather than rebel against it. Deleuze and Guattari rightly note that there is cunning in writing a letter to the father instead of fighting Oedipus directly. If private property were to disappear along with its seal, then why is it that beyond the seal the system is not even visible? This was a question that those who oppose phenomenon to essence should have been aware of. 

But what is the value of monogamy in the case of the immanence of the garment? It is merely a point of view, a dictate of assemblage, and a semiotic relation. After all, if I, like Proust, can generalize her under the shade of girls in flower as an image of that world, then what prevents me from linking her with any other sign? A woman is not a woman; she is a woman 

through certain relations and bonds. And this already allows us, within the family itself, to find lines of flight, molecular techniques. 

## **The State’s Cuck Chair, The Family, and The Social** 

Every time the _normal_ hetereo-monogamous sexual interaction occurs, an observant consents named as the state. 

A foucaultian cigarette here becomes harder to resist, sexuality is not repressed by the family so much as it is produced by it. It is merely inscribed into networks of discourse and power. The very notion, the very essence that monogamy presents itself does not end at restriction and protectionism, but a form of categorization, a mode of speaking and disciplinary desire. The family, even as a micro-cell of affection and reproduction, has another layer of relay in the state’s great machinery of regulating populations. The state’s non-centric, bottom-up power that spreads into the hair of every citizen, grasps the most private aspect of it all, that is emotional intimacy. The state becomes an observant of the sexual intercourse while continuously folding the participants back into itself. 

Monogamy is the state’s hammer that pins down inheritance, fixes paternity, and organizes different bodies into units of productivity, in a way that bodies become calculable. In this sense, the family is the bridge, the middle man, between the _anatomo-politics_ of the individual body and the _biopolitics_ of the population. To marry, to confess desire, to reproduce, are no longer a private matter, but investments in a wider economy of life and death. Ultimately, monogamy is the coding of sex into statistics. 

The family functions as the state’s hidden two-factor authentication (2FA), sex and reproduction ensure the _double confirmation_ of legitimacy. First, the confession of desire, the binding of two bodies under the sign of law (contract, marriage), this is the password. Later on, the reproductive trace, the child, the inheritance, the genealogy, becomes the code sent to the device of the state, confirming identity and passing access. To have monogamous sex is already to perform a biometric verification of the social body. The state is logged in through the flesh. To have sex under the regime of monogamy is already to perform two-factor authentication for the sovereign state. Once the password of desire is typed, offspring performs as confirmation code. The _successful login_ is called the family. 

In this imagery of the login and the double confirmation, Freud stands as the archivist of the confessions, every dream, slip, fantasy is entered into the state’s logbook. The unconscious itself becomes a kind of biometric verification: proof of the family’s inscription inside the psyche. The sacrifice of sexual freedom, the restriction held over the genitalia is the price that is paid for civilization, this is the ultimate Freudian cigar that one needs to smoke. 

The family reproduces, because it is an error-correction protocol, each child as a checksum, verifying the continuity of _the human_ against the pull of the inhuman outside. As the ultimate result, the family domesticates the sexual, using a hotspot from the ever-accelerating human libido to the human scale of apartments, surnames, mortgage payments, honeymoons, and birthday parties. 

For the tribals, or at least the children raised among extended family members in a communal sense, one can clearly breathe the monopsonistic molecules. This is in opposition to the exclusive monopolistic control that the married partnership holds over the child’s means of 

life, and the sibling’s rivalry for the scarce parent’s attention. The post-civilized sun screen seems to be hugging each skin, laid down, ready to be cut in pieces of category. 

We may understand that we despise fluidity for the sake of despising sexual fluidity, that our sexual monogamy haunts down and captures all other capacities into a series of isolated monos. One can say here, that the will which wants it all, is at first emanating from our sexual desire to want all of the sexual partner, an exclusive capture of the other body, other future, other fidelity. A form of totalitarian control and expansionism that ends at capital. This exclusive desire becomes a model for other relations. _Just as one partner is mine alone, property becomes mine alone, children become mine, lineage is mine_ . 

The logic of capture radiates outward. Monogamy thus provides an emotional and bodily training ground for ownership and exclusivity. Thus, capital owns the genitalia and unrolls itself through the entire veins. In such sense, the desire of the penis to own the vagina influences the desire of the buyer to have totalitarian control over the seller (monopsony), the desire of the buyer to control other buyers (monopoly), the desire of the voice to stand alone (monologues), the desire of the culture to dissect other cultures (monoculture), and the ultimate desire to be the sovereign, concentrated in a single body, that is, monarchy. Therefore, monism starts in the pants, and even if one ejaculates during the sexual discourse, there still exists beneath him, the desire to also ejaculate from the other monistic substances. 

Then, the economic system is located behind the pants. The exclusivity of sexual monogamy resonates with the exclusivity of property rights. The haunting of fluid desire (sexual openness, communal sharing) mirrors the suppression of commons, alternative economies, or non-possessive relations. The “monos” are mechanisms of reterritorialization, ways to code and 

striate desire, to make it calculable and governable. Monogamy here closes the flows of sex, monopoly closes the flows of exchange, monotheism closes the flows of belief, and monarchy closes the flows of power. The monos are the micro-templates of capitalism and the State. They train desire to accept capture. 

Mass media, assuming a prophetic role, through selective reading singles out from the disciplines the discourse of monogamy that proves most reproductive and advantageous within the current political situation. Yet since we, the perverted, aspire to be a militant margin in this vortex of molar productivity, we can confidently say that even **our monogamy deviates from your tune** , for we are able to apprehend the _il n’y a pas de femme_ of human desire without collapse. Then, our monogamy, the anarchic monogamy, finds its echo in the fluidity of the sexual union of the caveman. 

The anarchic couplehood here, the departed monogamy, is a mimicry of form, a mask, a way of smuggling other intensities (political, revolutionary, erotic) into the heart of bourgeois order. A couple who remains childless as a conscious refusal: fidelity without reproduction. A couple who re-routes reproductive energy into creative or revolutionary production: writing, art, militant politics. Two people can be faithful not because they’ve collapsed into sameness, but because their difference is inexhaustible, their encounter is a well of becoming. A Dionysian fidelity, _I am your labyrinth, I belong to you because I will throw myself into the abyss alongside you_ . A bond of excess, a force that creates outside the family unit. 

These few lines are open to misinterpretation. This essay is not aiming at the dissection and destruction of monogamy, but a basic critique of its usage in the state’s machine of control. 

## **The Transcendental** 

If every question arises from the problem of one and two, then it would be correct to discuss the problem of transcendent monogamy. 

Monogamous, that is to say, among all variable phenomena he grasps the unified and indivisible single _sex_ , towards which spiritual fidelity, and my own dependence as indivisible, stand far above the civil obligation toward any concrete person. In short: there are no women, there is _the Woman,_ who in more degraded perceptual fields is reproduced as a phenomenon. 

In the same way, polygamy does not mean _glory to the diversity of women_ , but rather the Nietzschean _life is a woman_ ; a dizzying diagram of garments and signs. This is not about private property or sexual identity, but about the _holy trinity_ of Jewish sex (Woman, Man, God) and about a _schizo-stroll_ . Thus, we redefine these two concepts anew. From the field of fidelity and quantity, we move them into the field of what may exist (the transcendent, the root) or may not exist (lightness, the immanent) behind the phenomenon (the garment), the object (in the first case hidden, in the second case the phenomenon itself). 

Let's not doubt that we post-moderns, with our thickly padded humanity that doesn’t want to knock against any stone, would be a comedy at which the tribals would laugh themselves to death. In fact, we are involuntarily funny, with our _intimate virtues_ . Monogamy is no longer transcendent but ridiculous, a comedy of paperwork and virtue-signaling. The _intimate virtues_ (fidelity, compromise, jealousy) are bourgeois protections, a cushion against the sharp encounter with multiplicity. 

So Person A and Person B can act as bricoleurs, tinkerers, for divine, political, and other incursions. In that case, there are two perspectives (fragile, hollow in themselves, and therefore all the more diverse). Either my partner, alongside me, is merely a conduit, not Bel, who rules and subdues, but Hayk, who turns his face away from such arrogance and, when necessary, fights. If I can see Dionysian liberation in their smile, what stops me from re-reading Marx in their conversation? She may be, by my hint, a woman, a woman in a secondary sense, a revolutionary micro-combatant who would carry _life, property, name_ ( Կյանք _,_ գույք _,_ անուն ) up to the border; or she may be my God-fearing fellow-servant and transmitter. And in all these cases, **we are bound by the marital seal of bureaucracy, whose reproductive control counts for nothing against the** **_black markets_ of our love.**
